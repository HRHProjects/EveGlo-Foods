EveGlo Foods launch delayed during ongoing Pacdora dispute

EveGlo Foods is temporarily delaying its product launch while our Alberta team addresses an ongoing dispute tied to the brand's packaging mockup process.

Launch notice April 28, 2026

EveGlo Foods is temporarily delaying its product launch due to an ongoing dispute with Pacdora, a packaging mockup and dieline platform used by creators, brands, and packaging teams.

This has been a difficult chapter for a small Alberta business working to bring a new health-focused pantry brand to market. EveGlo Foods was built around a clear product vision: low-carb rice alternatives, shirataki noodles, and high-protein pasta that make everyday meals easier for families, retail buyers, and health-minded kitchens.

This delay is not simply a scheduling issue. It has disrupted launch planning, product presentation, retail conversations, and the momentum required to introduce a new food brand responsibly. EveGlo Foods takes that disruption seriously and is working to protect the brand, the product vision, and the investment already made in preparing for market.

The impact also reaches beyond one launch date. Konjac-based foods are still early in their Canadian mainstream journey, and every credible new brand helps shoppers understand what shirataki rice and noodles can be, how they fit into everyday meals, and why they deserve space beside traditional pasta, rice, and other better-for-you pantry staples.

Canada has growing interest in low-carb, gluten-free, plant-forward, and lighter comfort-food options, but the category still needs education. A delay like this slows the work of introducing customers to konjac's practical benefits: rice and noodle alternatives that fit familiar meals, take on everyday seasonings, and offer a shelf-stable format for both online grocery discovery and retail placement.

For a smaller Alberta brand, timing matters. Launch windows create momentum with early customers, wholesale buyers, content partners, and community conversations. When a launch is forced to pause, that momentum becomes harder to maintain. EveGlo Foods does not view this disruption lightly, and the company remains focused on resolving the matter while preserving the standard customers and partners should expect.

EveGlo Foods will share updates about product availability, launch timing, and next steps in the coming weeks.

EveGlo Foods is owned and operated by Harmony Resource Hub Alberta Inc.
